Gareth Hughes is an art director, filmmaker & interactive designer

About me

After graduating with first class honours in Graphic Design, I joined Manchester's leading agency in interactive innovation, mN. Over three years I designed for Channel 4, The Co-operative, Arup and was part of the team who brought the award winning BBC Sport to mobile during the 2012 Olympics.

In 2014 I moved to London and joined AKQA where I focus on conceptual interactive design for new business as well as design lead on projects for multiple airlines, Studio Canal, MaxiNutrition, Nike and others…

Side projects

I'm a huge fan of side projects. They can touch on UI design, musical scores or writing and directing a short film.

Not long after after graduating I released 2‐Sugars; a simple utility iPhone app which was praised by Stephen Fry.

In 2012 I directed and scored Gloam, a short animated film which received overwhelming reception, surprisingly, and earned a Vimeo Staff Pick award.
