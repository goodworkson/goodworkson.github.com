Gloam

Gloam is a short animated film which depicts a solitary creature wandering a dark forest who encounters something unknown with only curiosity to lead the way.

Co-director, writer and composer

I wrote and co-directed Gloam with David Elwell who worked on the Animation. I also composed an original score for the short.

Originally intended as a showcase piece for David, a few hours after uploading, Gloam was featured by Vimeo and received over 100,000 views in the first month. We still don't believe it.

"A beautifully crafted short" – The Atlantic

"Better than titanic" – Youtube user

2 Sugars

2 Sugars is a tool that makes jotting down drink requests a doddle whether it's your round in a small team or a runner in an ad agency

Designer and co-creator

After creating the 2 Sugars 1.0 in 2010 shortly after graduating, I decided to give it a fresh look for 2.0 to demonstrate a smarter UX and more options to choose from.

Although 2 Sugars 2.0 isn't complete yet, v1.0 is still available on the app store.

Arup Explorer

A unique experience to explore over 600 projects by one of the largest engineering companies in the world.

Designer and UX

Using HTML5 Canvas to secure multi-platform usability to create an interactive experience that feels at home on a touch screen

Respecting current design guidelines while creating a alternative site view that sits parallel to arup.com.

People can collect projects that they find of interest which builds a unique ribbon of projects to share or save for later.

Displaying data was a focus for design: dozens of table types, text commentary and live streaming features.

University of Manchester

Large format information screens for the students of The University of Manchester.

Designer, UX

After openening in late 2012, the £24 million building allows students to work and socialise at any time of the day.

A suite of apps were created as part of a unique interface for students including a campus map for wayfinding, services and buildings information. A facility for locating computers and room availability in the building. Access to the story of the AGLC, live news and social media.

The outcome is simply a helpful experience that meets the expectations of students. Providing access to useful content and visually compliments the architecture.
